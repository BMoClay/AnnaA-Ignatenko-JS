Pendulum of Existence

Short Story

work in progress

Blake and Lexine, my two childhood best friends. Their father, Todd, worked at the Red Lion Inn, and their mom, Jill, was a busy stay-at-home pampered chef consultant. While my mom worked two jobs and was never home, my own solitude was frightening and also considered illegal. I found homage within their family unit, and they willfully took me in. I spent a lot of time with the Hagens throughout my elementary school days. My favorite dish by Jill was this questionable but yummy American/Chinese clash of melted cheddar cheese in white rice with soy sauce and sliced chicken, sometimes, she would switch out the soy sauce for this tangy BBQ sauce.

My entire upbringing is colored by trying to find homage in other homes and other families. This longing still exists, even in my thirties. By 5th grade, the cruel whims of childhood ostracization turned its gaze upon my friendship with Blake and Lexine. They, especially Blake, who was relegated to the 'special' group, were branded as 'other', a label I never understood. They were not 'other'; they were my refuge, my adopted family in a world where normalcy was a shifting, elusive shadow.
